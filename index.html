How it works

Four steps. Zero generic itineraries.

No more saving Pinterest boards that never become a walked Saturday. No more Google Maps' "popular places." A route built only for you, only for today.

1

Tell us what you love

Pick your moods — aesthetic cafés, vintage shops, street art, sunset spots, bookstores, hidden gardens. The AI learns your taste in 30 seconds.

2

Pick your time

Have 30 minutes or half a day? We match route length to your schedule — and to where you're standing right now.

3

Walk a route built for you

Turn-by-turn navigation through 5–8 hand-picked stops. Each one with the story of why we chose it for you specifically.

4

Save it as a Loop

Photos auto-organize into a sharable visual memory. Send it privately. Post it. Walk it again next month.

What makes it different

Aesthetic, not algorithmic.

Other apps optimize for ratings, traffic, or convenience. We optimize for your kind of beautiful.

AI-generated, never pre-curated

Every route is unique. Inventory is unbounded. The longer you use it, the better your recommendations get.

Mood-first, not rating-first

We index places by feel — vintage, cozy, hidden, sunset-friendly — not by a 4.6-star average. Your aesthetic is the signal.

Independent businesses, surfaced

Local cafés, galleries, and vintage shops we love — not the same chain restaurants every algorithm recommends.

The walk becomes a memory

Loops are visual artifacts of your day — a route, a story, a stack of photos. Share them, save them, walk them again.

Built privacy-first

Your taste profile never leaves your phone unencrypted. We process locations without selling them.

Designed for the city you're in

Manhattan-grade walking is different from suburban strolling. Routes are built around real neighborhood density, not raw distance.

Common questions

Things people ask first.

Is this for tourists or locals?

Both. Tourists use it to find the city behind the city. Locals use it to rediscover neighborhoods they thought they knew. Our most loyal beta users are New Yorkers who walk the same blocks every week and want a Sunday surprise.

How is this different from Google Maps?

Google Maps optimizes for proximity and traffic. We optimize for personal aesthetic. Maps are functional. Loops are emotional.

Why New York first?

It has the most walkable streets, the densest independent-business ecosystem, and the most demanding users. If a walking app works in New York, it works anywhere.

What does the AI actually do?

It takes your stated tastes, learns from what you actually click and walk, factors in your real-time location and time available, and picks 5–8 stops that sequence into a coherent walk. It doesn't invent places — every stop is from a curated, human-verified database.

Is it free?

Yes — three AI routes a month, free forever. Power users can upgrade to unlimited routes, ad-free walking, and group features.

I run a café/shop/gallery in NYC — how do I get featured?

Email mbkill4ever@gmail.com with the subject line "Charter business — [name]." Our charter program gives the first 30 partner businesses 90 days of free featured placement.

I'm an investor / partner — how do I get in touch?

Same email. Subject line "Walking Explorers — Investor / Partner." We'll send the deck and a link to the working beta.
